Three problems. One champion.
Medical Literacy
Your doctor said "bilateral adenotonsillar hypertrophy." Your discharge summary says "GERD with reflux oesophagitis." You nodded. But did you understand? Most Indians don't. And it's not their fault.
8 in 10 Indians have limited health literacy. They cannot fully understand their own diagnosis, prescription, or discharge summary.
Source: Medfin, citing Indian health literacy data
Limited health literacy is directly linked to medication errors, worse outcomes, and higher hospitalisation rates.
Source: PMC / REALM study, Coastal Karnataka
Fragmented Records
Five hospitals. Twelve doctors. Zero connected records. Every new doctor you see starts from scratch. Your history lives in paper files across different cities, clinics, and WhatsApp threads. The burden of connecting them falls on you.
Records scattered: prescriptions lost, medications recalled by colour or shape, not name.
Source: NITI Aayog FrontierTech, 2026
Preventable Errors
Most drug interaction errors don't happen because doctors are careless. They happen because no one gave the doctor the full picture. A medication you forgot to mention. An allergy not in the system. That's not a doctor problem. That's an information problem.
In Indian hospitals, 1 in 3 patients has at least one prescribing error. 68% are drug-drug interactions. Most preventable with a complete medication history.
Source: PMC / Chandigarh Government Medical College study
